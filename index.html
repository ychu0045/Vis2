COVID-19 IN MALAYSIA
Statistics from January 2020 to September 2021

Pandemic overview, where are we now?

As of September 2021, Malaysia has reported 2,245,695 confirmed cases and 26,708 deaths from COVID-19. Among all the states, Selangor is the worst hit in this pandemic. It has reported 677,664 confirmed cases and 9,408 deaths with an infection rate of 1,037 per 10,000 people.

How did the trend change overtime?

Tips: Click on the mark to highlight the trend of each state.

Overall, both confirmed cases and deaths are on the rise in most of the states. One of the reason is because of the Delta variant. It is airborne and so the transmission is faster. The first Delta variant case was detected in Sarawak on June 18, 2021.

How did the states rank in various aspects?

Tips: Click on the legend or mark to highlight the trend of each state.

The top 3 states with the highest number of confirmed cases are: Selangor, Kuala Lumpur and Sabah.
The top 3 states with the highest number of deaths are: Selangor, Sabah and Johor.

Tips: Click on the mark to highlight the statistics of each state.

Recovery rate is calculated by dividing the total recovered cases by the total confirmed cases as of September 2021. The mean recovery rate is 90.25%. Similarly, fatality rate is calculated by dividing the total deaths by the total confirmed cases as of September 2021. The mean fatality rate is 1.13%.

Note that Perlis has reported the lowest number of confirmed cases and deaths most of the time, but it has the lowest recovery rate and the highest fatality rate.
